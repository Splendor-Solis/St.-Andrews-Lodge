St. Andrews Masonic Lodge

St. Andrews Lodge is one of the first chartered Lodges back in 1805 and one of the oldest lodges of North Carolina.

We meet the second (2nd) Tuesday of every month at 7:30p.m. Dinner is at 6:30 p.m. & guests are welcomed.

Welcome

St. Andrews was chattered in 1805. This makes it one of the oldest Masonic Lodges in North Carolina.

  • Freemasonry aims to promote Friendship, Morality, and Brotherly Love among its members; men from every race, religion, opinion, and background who are brought together as Brothers to develop and strengthen the bonds of friendship.

  • Freemasonry proposes to "make good men better" by teaching - with metaphors from geometry and architecture - about building values based on great universal truths.

  • Finally, philanthropy and community service is fundamental to Freemasonry and something we actively take part in.
